Moonshot Team members: Arvind Girish, Aman Tyagi , Anish adamane Github Repo Link: https://github.com/arvindgirish-headout/project-moonshot What problem are you solving? Content is the single biggest lever for distribution today — text, images, and video are consumed at massive scale across YouTube, Instagram, and short-form platforms. We are firmly in the age of video, yet Headout has no scalable way to produce video content. There's growing skepticism around AI-generated video ("AI slop"), and rightly so. But the quality of AI video output is a function of two things: infrastructure (the underlying generation models) and orchestration (how you chain, prompt, and assemble those models into a finished product). We can't control infrastructure — a new breakthrough model could drop any week — but we can control orchestration. And because we ( Headout ) haven't invested in our video orchestration layer, we're falling behind. Project Moonshot is a platform that lets us create short-form video content (30–90 seconds) in one click. You select a POI, choose a duration, pick a theme, and the orchestration layer handles the rest. Three themes are built today: * Tour Guide — a guided walkthrough of the experience * Time Travel — go back in history and experience the POI in its original era * Cinematic — a story-driven, suspense-laced narrative around the experience The platform will soon add a feedback layer with human review gates and deeper customization controls. What metric does this move, and why does that metric matter? Primary metrics: Repeat rate, social sharing, and customer LTV. Why these matter: The first product test sends AI-generated videos (as YouTube links) to customers post-booking via WhatsApp Meta templates for top-selling POIs. The hypothesis is that delightful, personalized video content about the experience they've booked increases engagement and emotional connection — which converts into higher repeat purchase rates, organic social sharing, and ultimately greater lifetime value. Proxy metric for the experiment: YouTube link open rate from WhatsApp messages. If customers who have already paid are opening and watching these videos, it validates that the content creates incremental delight beyond the transactional confirmation. If you nail it, what materially changes? (put a number on it if you can) Short term (0–4 weeks): Video content for top-selling POIs goes from zero to dozens — produced in minutes, not weeks. Today creating a single 60-sec POI video requires a creative brief, stock footage sourcing, editing, and review — easily 5–10 hours of human effort per video. Moonshot collapses this to under 5 minutes. If you generate 3 themes × 50 top POIs = 150 videos in the first sprint, that's ~750–1,500 hours of creative work replaced. The WhatsApp post-booking experiment targets a 15–25% video open rate (WhatsApp template CTRs typically run 15–45%), giving you a statistically significant signal on delight within a few thousand sends. Medium term (1–3 months): If the delight hypothesis holds, even a 1–2% lift in repeat rate across high-volume POIs compounds meaningfully at Headout's scale. The same orchestration layer pivots to automated ad creative via hyperframes + Keygen — a single performance marketer could generate and A/B test 10–20 video ad variants per POI per week instead of waiting days for one. Direct ROAS improvement through creative iteration speed. Long term (3–6 months): The orchestration layer becomes horizontal infrastructure — any video need (ads, social reels, in-app previews, email assets, SP pitch videos) is one click. When the next-gen model drops (Sora, Kling, Runway Gen-4, whatever), you plug it in and every video across the entire catalogue improves overnight. That's the moat: competitors who start building orchestration then are 6 months behind.
